Frédéric François Chopin

Frédéric François Chopin, born in 1810, was a gifted Polish pianist and composer of the romantic period. He studied piano and harmony at a young age, composing his first piece at age 7 and giving his first concert at age 8. Although he was born in Poland, he spent most of his adult life in Paris, building a network among the wealthy as a prominent piano teacher. He left an influential mark on his contemporaries who considered his teaching methods revolutionary, helping to shape modern piano teaching as it is today. As a pianist and composer, he is best known for his solo works and piano concerti. A shy man, Chopin only ever played piano in the dark, only performing 30 public concerts in his lifetime.
